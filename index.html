How to Shoot a Basketball

Basketball is one of the most popular sports in the world, and also one of my favorites. Its charm comes from simplicity and complexity being meshed together in a fast paced, scoring filled game. One of the most important aspects in basketball is being able to shoot the basketball accurately and efficiently. Being confident with your shot is a vital part of being a basketball player. Although originally said by a hockey player, Wayne Gretzky's famous quote applies to basketball as well:You miss 100% of the shots you don't take.

The Process

One important thing to keep in mind when learning proper shooting form is that there is no truly "correct" way to shoot a basketball. For example, some superstars like Hall of Famer Larry Bird had very unorthodox, unnatural shooting motions. This did not stop him from being one of the best shooters to ever play basketball.

The first major thing in shooting is keeping a good stance. This means keeping your dominant eye and arm lined up with the basket, as well as keeping feet pointed in direction of basket with legs in a comfortable position around shoulder lenght apart. The next thing to learn about is the shot pocket. This is the place where you bring the ball after catching a pass or stopping your dribble. You should always try to grip and raise the ball the same way every time you shoot.

The final part and most noticeable is the actual shot itself. It starts with bending your knees to create less stress on the arms and jump. You need to flick the wrist in a natural motion toward the basket with your dominant hand, with the other hand supporting the ball without changing the arc of the ball. While you shoot, your elbow should remain facing the basket at close to a 90 degree angle. You should flick the wrist near the top of your jump for best results.

Conclusion

Basketball is a fun game. I hope this little site gave you a bit more insight on how to master an important part of the game. To Recap, the steps we went over today were:

  • Be Comfortable
  • Be Square to the Basket
  • Establish a Comfortable Shot Pocket
  • Flick Wrist at the Top of your Jump

These are not all you will need to learn, however. Visit sites like Breakthrough Basketball to help learn how to further improve.
